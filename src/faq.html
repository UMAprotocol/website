About UMA

What does UMA stand for?

UMA stands for universal market access.

What is UMA's mission?

UMA’s mission is to make financial markets universally accessible—unrestrained by censorship, pre-existing financial and social capital, or beliefs and values. The economic freedom created by universal market access creates equal opportunity to pursue prosperity and build financial independence .

What is a synthetic asset?

Synthetic assets are collateral-backed tokens whose value fluctuates depending on the tokens’ reference index. They are created by depositing collateral into a smart contract and minting tokens backed by that collateral. One example of a synthetic token is Defi TVL, an ERC-20 token that tracks the total value locked in Defi.

Priceless Contracts

What are priceless financial contracts?

UMA provides priceless financial contract templates for developers to easily create synthetic tokens.

Up until now, synthetic token designs have required smart contracts to know the value of its collateral at all times, as reported by an on-chain price feed. “Priceless” synthetic tokens differ because they do not require an on-chain price feed to indicate whether the contract is properly collateralized. Instead, they have a liquidation mechanism that allows anyone to liquidate an undercollateralized position.

In this design, liquidators can choose to liquidate positions based on their own off-chain view of the reference index for the token, which informs their view on whether the position is properly collateralized. Positions that have not been liquidated are assumed to be properly collateralized.

Liquidators are kept honest by disputers who are rewarded for catching invalid liquidations. Only when a liquidation is disputed is an oracle needed. UMA’s priceless financial contract templates leverage a decentralized oracle service known as the Data Verification Mechanism (DVM). This system of incentives functions to minimize oracle usage while keeping the entire system correctly collateralized.

Data Verification Mechanism

What is UMA’s Data Verification Mechanism (DVM)?

UMA uses an oracle only when there is a dispute versus other oracle solutions that constantly push prices on-chain. Minimizing oracle usage increases the security and scalability of decentralized financial contracts.

The DVM is a decentralized oracle service used to resolve disputes of liquidations and to settle synthetic token contracts upon expiration. UMA token holders are incentivized to respond to price disputes by referencing the index price off-chain and reporting the price to the DVM. This price is used as an input by the DVM to determine the final price of the asset.

If the price returned by the DVM determines the disputer was correct, the disputer is rewarded with a penalty charged to the liquidator; conversely if the disputer was incorrect, the disputer forfeits a bond to the liquidator.

Regulation Considerations

Are there regulatory risks when creating synthetic assets?

UMA is censorship agnostic enabling anyone to create their own synthetic token as long as the collateral and price identifier has been approved by UMA token holders. Certain synthetic tokens may violate regulations in particular jurisdictions. Do your own research prior to offering synthetic tokens to users.
