Call/Put Options

A Call/Put option gives the buyer the right, but not the obligation to buy or sell an asset at a specified strike price and a specific time period.

Why create call / put options?

Utilize idle tokens in treasuries or investor portfolios to earn premiums.

Distribute airdrops via options that prevent against immediate token sell-offs while incentivizing long-term holders and community members.

Strengthen communities via rewarding community members and contributors with options engrained with long-term.

Offer products with leveraged speculation to investors and community members with limited downside.

How is it better?

No risk of liquidation or need to manage collateralization ratios.

Settlements occurs two hours after expiry (if there are no disputes).

Cash settled — At expiry, the sponsor of the options contract will pay any profit due to the holder, rather than take payment from the tokenholder.

European style options — the call options can only be exercised at expiry.

Examples

A trader buys a call option with a strike price of $2,000 for 0.05 ETH. They have the right to buy 1 ETH for $2,000. At the expiry, ETH/USD is at $2,500 and the delivery price is $2,500. In this case, the option is settled for $500 per 1 ETH. The trader’s account is credited with 0.2 ETH ($500/$2,500), and the seller’s account is debited with 0.2 ETH. The initial purchase price was 0.05 ETH; therefore, the trader’s profit is 0.15 ETH.

A DAO Treasury sells a call option with a strike price of 2,000 USD for 0.05 ETH. The delivery price at expiry is 1,999 USD. The call option expires worthless. The buyer lost 0.05 ETH, and the Treasury gained 0.05 ETH.
